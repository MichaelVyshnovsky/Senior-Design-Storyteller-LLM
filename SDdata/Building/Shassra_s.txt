Shassras was one of the best known boarding houses in Arabel, Cormyr as of 1368 DR.
Renowned for its cleanliness, Shassras was furnished with simple wares that provided a welcoming atmosphere.
The boarding house welcomed folks from all walks of life, including less savory types.
Guests had the option to rent private rooms at a rate of 4 copper pieces per night or shared rooms for half that price. A bath would cost a single silver piece. The only amenities provided for rooms were a ewer of drinking water, a basin of rinsing water, and a chamberpot.
