'''Amnian phrases''' are those with specific meaning in [[Amn]]. There are a number of these in common usage.
__NOTOC__

===B===
'''Book''' &ndash; Used with an adjective such as clean, other or true, to imply that someone is being dishonest. ''What is in your clean book?'' is a request for the truth, as are ''give me the true book'' and ''read from your other book''. The terms refer to a corrupt business which keeps two copies of account books.

'''Buy''' &ndash; Used in the place of 'accept', of a deal, explanation or plan.

===C===
'''Crimson [[ink]]''' &ndash; Very bad luck. See also [[#R|red ink]].

===D===
'''Dragon''' &ndash; Can be used to describe a person who is miserly, although sensible, with their wealth. The term implies respect.

===F===
'''Finance''' &ndash; To gain something beyond one's monetary means or something undeserved.

'''Foreclose''' &ndash; A very loose term that can mean a variety of things depending on context and intent, including stop, take over, steal, hijack or kill.

===G===
'''Good business''' &ndash; A general expression that can mean 'hello' or 'goodbye'.

===K===
'''Kobold''' &ndash; A derogatory term used to imply stupidity.

===O===
'''Outbid''' &ndash; To beat or be more impressive.

===P===
'''Pearl''' &ndash; Used to personify luck. To ''find the pearl'' is to be lucky, and respectively, ''lose the pearl'' is to be unlucky. A lucky gain can be called a ''delivery by pearl''.

===R===
'''Red [[ink]]''' &ndash; Bad luck. ''The ink couldn't get redder'' is used to describe a situation where things could not be worse. See also [[#C|crimson ink]].

===S===
'''Sell''' &ndash; Used in the place of 'convince', for example: "you've sold me!" Can be misinterpreted literally, leading to the belief that slavery is condoned in Amn.

===T===
'''Take delivery''' &ndash; One who takes delivery of something is one who acquires something through skill or work.

===Y===
'''You're only as tall as your last deal''' &ndash; Used to imply that one cannot rely on notoriety for long. Can be used to warn that the recipient has not done much for the speaker lately.
