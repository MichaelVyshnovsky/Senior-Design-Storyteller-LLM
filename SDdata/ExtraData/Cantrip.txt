"'''Cantrip'''" was the common name for the weakest spells an [[arcane]] spellcaster could cast. The [[divine magic]] equivalents were known as [[orison]]s, but "cantrip" was often used to describe all beginner apprentice/acolyte spells. Cantrips were the first spells that a wizard learned during their apprenticeship.

The effects that could be created were very minor, very brief, and mostly harmless, intended to entertain children or impress commoners,

==Realmslore==
* The mage [[Sabirine]] was well known to have had a special interest in cantrips, and was an enthusiastic advocate of the continued use and refinement of them. Sabirine created several unique cantrips including ''catfeet'', ''snatch'', and ''spark''.{{Cite dragon/100/Pages from the Mages V|12-13}}
* ''[[The Alcaister]]'', a spellbook with a dangerous reputation, contained two pages that described the cantrips ''cut'', ''gallop'', and ''sting''.{{Cite dragon/100/Pages from the Mages V|18-19}}
* The [[spellbook]] known as ''[[Briel's Book of Shadows]]'' contained a description of the ''exterminate'', ''smokepuff'', ''tie'', and ''wilt'' cantrips.{{Cite dragon/97/Pages from the Mages IV|34}}
* The spellbook penned by [[Glanvyl]] of [[Scornubel]] contained descriptions of ''listen'', ''horn'', and ''scorch''.{{Cite dragon/100/Pages from the Mages V|14}}
