This page lists common '''oaths, idioms, and colloquialisms''' in the Realms, those that are not specific to any faith or culture.

==Oaths==
; Bind me and tar me
: Usually shortened to "Bind me!" A roughly and slightly stronger equivalent of "Strike me down!"

; By the great mystery 
: This was exclaimed when one witnessed something magical or unnatural. [[Jalma]] cried this when [[chasme]] [[demon]]s burst through her window{{Cite comic/The Gathering|10}} and [[Vajra Valmeyjar]] swore it when she saw a [[basilisk]] turned to stone.{{Cite comic/The Bounty Seekers Of Manshaka|22}} <ref group="note">This may be a reference to the goddess of magic [[Mystra]], called the Lady of Mysteries.

; By the sainted dog
: Vajra gasped this when [[Priam Agrivar]] healed her injuries and woke her.{{Cite comic/Sorcerer's Moon|8}}

; Dark and empty
: Usually shortened to "Dark!" A roughly equivalent of "Damn and blast!"

; Happy dancing hobgoblins
: A mildly comical oath, once popular but used almost exclusively by older people in the late 15{{th}} century [[DR]], usually when astonished. [[Hobgoblin]]s did not say this, and were not amused when they hear it.

; Lolth's bloated thorax
: An exclamation of surprise that was occasionally uttered by [[drow]].

; Those of all the [[Nine Hells]] take you!
: Usually shortened to "Hells!". It began as the roughly equivalent of "The devil take you!", but by the 15{{Th}} century was more closer to "Hell take us all."

; I'll turn out your guts for blood red garters!
: An aggressive oath, said by those usually looking for a fight. Usually shortened to "Red garters!".

; Rhyester's Eyes!
: An exclamation of surprise, as in "Rhyester's Eyes! What a fighter he was." It was derived from the famous once-blind prophet [[Rhyester]].

; Swear by the [[oak]] and the unicorn's horn
: A common expression among [[druid]]s from [[Sossal]] when making a promise.

==Idioms==
; A proper earner
: Was said in [[Crimmor]] of a ''very nice'' tool or implement. Such tools would impress Crimmans very much.{{Cite dragon/334/Crimmor: City of Caravans|22-34}}

; Being caught in crimson
: Idiom used in [[Crimmor]] to talk about a thief caught red-handed.

; Being on Vergadain's crusade
: An idiom used in the [[Sword Coast]] to refer [[dwarves]] that were overly drunk, referencing the god [[Vergadain]].<ref>{{Cite adventurers league/Durlag's Tomb|12}}

; Calling an all-horns hunt
: Idiom used by the [[Waterdhavian Watch]], when releasing a bulletin to all guards in order to look for a dangerous or missing person. The term makes reference to the signal horns carried by all officers on patrol.{{Cite dragon/413/Eye on the Realms: The Six Horned Crowns}}

; Coin kept at home is begging to be stolen
: A common [[Waukeen]]ar saying about not using money.

; Flush with high-heaped coins
: An idiom from the [[Sword Coast]] describing an individual or a group (e.g. [[trading company]], [[guild]], family) who were eager to invest or to buy large quantities of goods; usually to start a new endeavor or to expand their portfolio.

; Guarding mine/his glints
: Idiom used in [[Crimmor]] meaning to hide mine/his valuable wares from public eyes to avoid having them stolen after leaving the city.

; Having too little coin to buy their own confidence
: Was used in [[Crimmor]] to talk about someone ostensibly flaunting their money by owning unnecessary ornate buildings or clothing.

; He/she counts like a halfling
: Someone is just being difficult for no good reason. This was a reference to how [[halfling]]s were known to count the days of the week starting with their pinky fingers instead of starting with their thumbs like everyone else.

; Hoist by your own petard
: A common prophetic proverb or warning that an action, device, plan, plot, or scheme that someone is enacting will negatively backfire on them.<ref>

; Interrupt one's current
: An idiom among the people of [[Ser√¥s]] that referred to someone as getting physically too close to another or getting in someone else's way.<ref>

; Men fall, but Tempus rides on!
: An older saying among the followers of [[Tempus]]. In disuse by the 15{{Th}} century.

; Orcs is always trouble
: An old upcountry saying about [[orc]]s and their ilk, including [[half-orc]]s. Usually used by [[human]]s, [[elves]] and [[dwarves]]. In the 15{{Th}} century was usually shortened to "Orcs is trouble".

; Riding mine/his own coin
: An idiom from the [[Sword Coast]] and [[Western Heartlands]] which meant "At mine/his own expense". It could also be used to mean "I'll pay for this round".

; Swirlcloaks
: Families in [[Waterdeep]] that aspire to become nobles by manner of dress, by attending social gatherings where nobles are present, and by buying noble favor.{{Cite dragon/274/The New Adventures of Volo: The Stag Lass|108}}

; There's a special place in the Hells for that one
: A common idiom used to express low opinion of someone. Variations of the phrase substituted Hells for either "Hell", "the Nine Hells", or "the Myriad Pits". Among intellectuals, the word "Baator", the proper name for the Nine Hells in [[infernal language|Infernal]], would commonly be used instead.

; Utter discretion, with coded receipts
: A common Waukeenar saying about doing discreet business, also used by [[Manycoins services|moneylenders]].
