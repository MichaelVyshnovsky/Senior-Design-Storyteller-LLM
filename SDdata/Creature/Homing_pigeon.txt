'''Homing pigeons''', also known as '''carrier pigeons''' or '''messenger pigeons''', were a domesticated species of [[pigeon]] specially trained to return to a particular location when released into the sky.

==Habitats==
Frequently moving [[halfling]] communities in the [[Marching Mountains]] would keep in touch with each other through use of messenger pigeons.

Homing pigeons were found in [[Shou Lung]] and in much of [[Zakhara]]. In the bazaars of Zakhara, the price of a homing pigeon varied from around 75 to 150 [[gold piece]]s depending upon how good one was at haggling. Homing pigeons were also found in the [[Feywild]].<ref>

==Notable Homing Pigeons==
* [[Commander Lightfeather]], a carrier pigeon that worked for the [[Sword Coast Couriers]] in [[Rivington]] in the late 15{{th}} century [[DR]].

==Notable Users==
===General===
* Some [[bandit]]s utilized carrier pigeons.<ref>

===Businesses===
* [[The Worried Wyvern|Worried Wyvern Inn]] ran a messenger pigeon service.<ref>
* Sword Coast Couriers, a courier business in the Rivington district of [[Baldur's Gate]], used carrier pigeons.
===Individuals===
* [[Melannor Fellbranch]] was partial to contacting members of the [[Emerald Enclave]] through means of carrier pigeons.

* [[Ting Mei Wan]] and her agents utilized homing pigeons.

===Organizations===
[[File:Cormyr pigeon post.jpg|250px|thumb|''A messenger pigeon post in [[Eveningstar]].'']]
* The [[Everlasting]], the largest [[holy slayer]] organization in Zakhara, widely utilized homing pigeons,<ref> keeping them in an aviary atop the roof of the [[Tower of Martyrs]] in [[Sarahin]].
* The [[Knights of Myth Drannor]] used homing pigeons.
* Members of the [[Lords' Alliance]] communicated via trained carrier pigeons, provided by [[Piergeiron Paladinson]] of [[Waterdeep]].<ref><ref>
* In [[Zhentarim]] garrisons, messenger pigeons were one of the means that secondary guards often had for reporting to Zhents elsewhere.<ref>
* The kingdom of [[Cormyr]] used pigeons to deliver messages in the 15{{th}} century DR.
