'''Anteos''' ({{Pronounce|AN|tee|oh|s|refs=}}) was a [[Nobility of Waterdeep|noble family]] within the city of [[Waterdeep]] during the 14{{th}} century [[DR]].

==Organization==
House Anteos was ennobled in the {{YearlinkName|1248}} when the [[Lords of Waterdeep]] recognized several merchant houses within the city's nobility.

As of the Year of the Prince, [[1357 DR]] [[Dulbravvan Anteos|Dulbravvan]] was leader and patriarch of the family. Dulbravvan's younger brother, [[Korras Anteos]], was replaced as heir by [[Dulbravvan Anteos II|Dulbravvan II]], Dulbravvan and [[Ranaya Anteos|Ranaya's]] infant son, prior to [[1372 DR]].{{Cite web enhancement/Noble Houses of Waterdeep|2}}

==Activities==
They were prominent traders and money changers throughout the region, but had in the past had earned their fortune through slavery.

==Base of Operations==
The family had [[Anteos Villa|an estate]] in the [[North Ward of Waterdeep|North Ward]] of the city between [[Anteos Lane]] and [[Trollkill Street]].

==History==
The same year the house was ennobled, the Year of the Cockatrice ([[1248 DR]]), they were requested to renounce their slave trading and their participation in it. House Anteos were forced once again, upon the restoration of the Lordsâ€™ rule, to renounce slave trading in the Year of
the Wagon ([[1273 DR]]).

Prior to [[1357 DR]], Dulbravvan and Ranaya had a stillborn son. During this time Dulbravvan's younger brother was made temporary heir before their infant son, Dulbravvan II, was born and took his place as heir to the house by [[1372 DR]].
