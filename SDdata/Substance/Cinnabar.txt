'''Cinnabar''' was an ore of [[quicksilver]] that was used decoratively, in magic [[spell]]s and rituals, and in [[poison]]making. 

==Properties==
Cinnabar was a soft, red stone, with a weight of {{SI|506|pound|k|long=yes}} per {{SI|1|cubic foot|l|long=yes}}, making it one of the heaviest minerals.{{Cite dragon/152/In a Cavern, In a Canyon...|23}}

==Availability==
* Cinnabar could be found in the form of a rich red sand, often near hot springs and volcanoes.

==Usages==
[[File:Vermilion Dye.png|thumb|200px|''A pot of cinnabar-based fabric dye.'']]
* Cinnabar was processed for the metal [[mercury]] present in it.
* Cinnabar was ground down and used as to make rich vermilion [[dye]] for fabric.
* Cinnabar could be used as a material to create various decorations and runes.
* Cinnabar was used as an ingredient in poisons.
* In [[Waterdeep]], carved cinnabar jewelry, such as brooches, could be seen worn by the wealthy.
* In [[Halruaa]], [[wizard]]s developed a spell that conjured ''[[cinnabar rain]]'' that possessed certain ''[[dispelling]]'' properties. 

===Spell Components===
* As a [[spell component]], cinnabar was used in castings of ''[[animate water]]'',{{Cite dragon/130/Arcane Lore: Magic from East to West|18}} ''[[animate wood]]'', and ''[[surelife]]''.
* In the land of the [[Red Wizards]], [[Thay]], cinnabar was used in overly complex [[arcane]] spells for the small quantities of quicksilver in the mineral. When used together with [[mustard oil]], it was converted to quicksilver, which allowed for additional reflective and translocative ''[[dweomer]]s''.
