'''Pitch''', or '''tar''', was a black plant [[resin]] that was used by laborers and [[spell]]casters.{{Cite dragon/209/The Dragon's Bestiary: The golem workshop|89-90}} It had some similarities with various plant and vegetable [[oil]]s, [[rock oil]], and [[bitumen]].
{{quote
|I find it ironic that even our most lofty edifices are held together by ground [[horse]] hooves, sandy lime, and foul-smelling tar.
|Excerpt from ''[[Aurora's Whole Realms Catalogue]]''
}}

==Properties==
Pitch was a highly flammable liquid.{{Cite dragon/97/Sticks, stones, and bones|15}} It weighed {{SI|67|pound|k|long=yes}} per {{SI|1|cubic foot|l|long=yes}}.{{Cite dragon/13/How Heavy Is My Giant|6}}

==Availability==
[[File:Spruce Resin.png|thumb|''Resin from a spruce tree.'']]
* Pitch was refined from tree resin, most commonly from [[spruce]]s and [[pine]]s, and mixed with ash from large, hot fires. The different materials used could give the pitch different qualities such as smell and color.{{Cite web/Candlekeep Forum/Questions for Ed Greenwood (2010)/08-01 - Petroleum Oil in the Realms}}
* Pitch was stored in containers such as bottles and [[barrel]]s, and often sold along with [[glue]] and [[mortar]].
* ''[[Aurora's Whole Realms Catalogue (in-universe)|Aurora's Whole Realms Catalogue]]'' sold bottles of pitch for 5 [[copper piece]]s per {{SI|1|quart|c}}.

==Usage==
* Pitch was used in bulk for securing roof tiles and waterproofing leaky roofs.{{Cite web/Elminster Speaks archive|73-74}}{{Cite web/Elminster Speaks/Part 57: Khôltar, Part 8, A First Look at Handrornlar}}{{Cite dragon/299/Elminster's Guide to the Realms: Moon Mountain Brewery|80}}
* Containers of pitch could set alight and thrown as weapons, splashing targets with the thick, burning liquid.
* Pitch could be used as a sort of [[paint]].
* [[Hip boots]], which were also available from ''Aurora's Whole Realms Catalogue'', were completely sealed in pitch to make them waterproof.
* Pitch was a key ingredient in producing strong [[putty]].
* Sweet-smelling pitch was used to make [[incense]].
* [[Lacquer]] was made using pitch.
* Wooden furniture was sometimes put together with pitch.
* Some medicinal oils used pitch as an ingredient, and wound packings.
* [[Tar makeup]] was made of tar, prepared to avoid harming the wearer's skin.
* Pitch was used by tanners working hides.{{Cite dragon/98/Knowing what's in store|32}}
* Pitch was used in the preparation of [[dragon turtle]] scales for use in armor.
* The wood of [[laspar]] trees were thick with pitch, and attempting to light it caused sparking. It was often used in [[candle]]s and [[torch]]es.

===Spell Components===
Pitch was very commonly used as a [[material component]] for spellcasting, particularly for spells that involved darkness. Some of the spells requiring pitch were: 
* ''[[Improved spider climb]]'', ''[[darkness]]'', ''[[enhance fire creature]]'', ''[[continual darkness]]'', and ''[[maddening darkness]]''.
* Pitch was used by [[mage]]s to create or empower fire-based creatures. [[Burning man]] [[golem]]s required pitch as an essential part of their construction. 

==History==
* Those of the [[Flamedeath Fellowship]] used [[flame knife|flame knives]] that were covered in pitch.{{cite book/Assassin Mountain/Holy Slayer Sourcebook|4}}
* In the old days of [[Cormyr]], the King's champion [[Grymmar]] used flaming pitch to corral an [[ochre jelly]] into a stone coffin that he then sealed with pitch. He then transported it to meet an army of bandits, and proceeded to use it to destroy the invading force.{{Cite dragon/104/Ecology of the Ochre Jelly|33-36}}
