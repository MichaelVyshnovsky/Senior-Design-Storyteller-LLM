Platinum was a rare silvery-white metal sometimes used as a currency in the Realms.  Its value was generally greater than gold.
Larger and more powerful cities and states used platinum pieces as currency throughout the ages. Most commonly, Cormyr, Waterdeep,
and Amn would mint and circulate platinum currency within their own cities. In contrast, platinum pieces were not regularly used in Zakhara and were very rare to find.
Platinum was often used in decoration as a sign of wealth and status. One examples of this was King Osaw Is use of platinum decoration on his armor to represent his rank as king.
Platinum was a heavy yet malleable metal which resembled aluminum in appearance.
While rarer than other grades of coin, platinum was most commonly used in currency. It was also used in crafting such uses include armor, weapons, jewelry, & magic items as well as the casting of some spells which required platinum as components. Platinum was also in construction and in decoration.
Platinum was even used as food by Foo Dogs but this was not common among other creatures.

While platinum coins were reasonably rare across the Forgotten Realms, they were popular among adventurers and in busier trade cities.
In Cormyr platinum coins were known as tricrowns, while in the Windrise Ports a platinum piece was called a splendor.
Elves within Cormanthyr would trade in fine platinum pieces they called ruendils.Other coins found within the empire included Myth Drannors yulthaari five-inch-long metal tubes crafted of platinum. These were worth the equiverlent of three platinum pieces due to their metal content. Myth Drannor also traded and used platinum coins shaped into a teardrop called sheelba.
The roldon was a planinum coin used in Amn, informally referred to as the pearl. While it was technically used as currency, it was noted that using these coins was a show of wealth.
In Calimshan, a variety of individual mints and coins were used and traded interchangeably, including a variety of platinum pieces. These included Calimports kilarche coin, Manshakas djendjen coin, Volothamps mandrille, & Keltars redoline piece.
