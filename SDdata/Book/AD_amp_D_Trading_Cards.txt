'''''AD&D Trading Cards''''' or '''''Collector Cards''''' were a series of trading cards published by [[TSR, Inc.]] between 1991 and 1993. The cards adhere to 2nd-edition ''[[Dungeons & Dragons|Advanced Dungeons &amp; Dragons]]'' rules, and were released in three separate yearly series.

==Description==
The ''AD&D Trading Cards'' were released in three yearly sets between 1991 and 1993. Each set consists of two different series, and the 1992 and 1993 sets feature additional promotional and rare cards in addition to the main set.
*''1991 Trading Cards'': 750 cards
*''1992 Trading Cards'': 750 + 11 promotional cards
*''1993 Collector Cards'': 495 + 60 rare cards

===Cards===
[[File:AD&D 1992 GenCon 7.jpeg|thumb|200px|Example of an individual card, featuring [[Yvonnel Baenre|Matron Mother Baenre]] from the 1992 GenCon series.]]
Each individual card has the dimensions 63 × 88 mm (2.5 × 3.5 inches), and is printed on cardboard,{{Cite card/AD&D Trading Cards/1991|1}}{{Cite card/AD&D Trading Cards/1993|495}} with the exception of promotional cards included with issues of [[Dragon (magazine)|''Dragon'' magazine]].{{Cite dragon/160/AD&D Trading Cards!}}

The front of each card features art, either re-used from other TSR products, or original to the ''Trading Cards''.{{Cite dragon/184/The Game Wizards: Secrets of a trading card guru}} The art is outlined by a thick, solid-color border, the color of which varies depending on series and/or rarity.

==History==
The ''AD&D Trading Cards'' were first introduced by TSR employee [[James Ward]] in [[Dragon magazine 160|issue #160 of ''Dragon'' magazine]], as an insert of 17 cards. Mostly based on popular characters from various ''AD&D'' novels and video games, this insert of "rookie cards" was intended to test the waters for a potential trading card product line. If the ''Dragon'' insert proved popular, Ward wanted to release yearly series of cards, featuring both popular TSR artwork and characters, as well as characters and creatures found nowhere else.

These trading cards were followed by a collectible card game (CCG) announced in the August 1994 issue of Dragon magazine ([[Dragon magazine 207|Dragon #207]]) called ''[[Spellfire: Master the Magic]]''. It included cards based on many of TSR's game worlds, including the Forgotten Realms, Dark Sun, and Greyhawk settings.{{Cite dragon/207/The Game Wizards: The SPELLFIRE card game}}
