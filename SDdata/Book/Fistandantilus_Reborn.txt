'''''Fistandantilus Reborn''''', by [[Douglas Niles]], is the second novel in [[The Lost Gods]] trilogy. Unlike the other entries in the series, ''Fistandantilus Reborn'' takes place on the world of [[Krynn]] in the [[Dragonlance]] setting, but characters and events from the novel are carried over into the [[Forgotten Realms]]-set sequel ''[[Tymora's Luck]]''.

{{Quote|'''Explore two beloved Dungeons & Dragons campaign settings&mdash;Dragonlance and the Forgotten Realms&mdash;in this thrilling crossover novel by the author of ''Darkwalker on Moonshae''.'''
<br />
Emilo Haversack is unusually worried for a kender&mdash;though he can't for the life of him remember what his problem is. Still, he's been wandering across Ansalon for quite some time, trying to remember what’s wrong. All he can recall is the name of a very evil, very dead wizard: Fistandantilus.
<br />
Joined by a young kendermaid, a human lad, and a devout, if somewhat naive priest, Emilo is drawn into a mysterious quest. As he draws closer to the secret of his past, he and his friends find themselves caught in an increasingly sinister web of intrigue. As the conclusion of the adventure approaches, the companions learn that their purpose is one that could catapult them from Krynn&mdash;and forever change the destiny of the world they leave behind.}}
