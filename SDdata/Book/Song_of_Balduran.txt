The '''"Song of Balduran"''', also known as '''"The Elf Song"''', was a melodramatic ballad written some time before the late 15{{th}} century [[DR]], dedicated to the city of [[Baldur's Gate]]'s namesake, [[Balduran]].
==Contents==
{{quote|''O, sing a song of Balduran''<br/>''Who founded Baldur's Gate.''<br/>''Empire golden built on trade,''<br/>''Could not avert his fate.''<br/><br/>''When three, though dead, assailed his port''<br/>''Transformed, he fell their thrall.''<br/>''Succumbed as threat from nether years''<br/>''Arose to conquer all.''<br/><br/>''Now hope is gone, or so it seems''<br/>''But game's not over yet.''<br/>''New cards are drawn, new hands are played''<br/>''Newcomers place a bet.''<br/><br/>''A [[rogue|knave]], a [[wizard]], [[devil]], [[gith]]:''<br/>''The odds are cast anew''<br/>''And Baldur's fate now turns upon''<br/>''The whims of fortune's few...''|The ''Song of Balduran''.}}

==History==
In the {{YearlinkName|1492}}, the ''Song of Balduran'' could be heard sung A [[book]] that recorded "The Elf Song"'s lyrics was stored in the tavern's second floor, which was reserved to be used as guest accommodations.
