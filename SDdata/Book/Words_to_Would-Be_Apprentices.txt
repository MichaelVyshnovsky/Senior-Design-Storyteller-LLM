'''''Words to Would-be Apprentices''''' was a [[book]] penned by [[Khelben Arunsun]], the Blackstaff of [[Waterdeep]] in the {{YearlinkName|1332}}.

{{quote|''Know this, mageling, and know it well: there is no greater fool than a [[wizard]]. The greater the mage, the greater the fool, because we who work magic live in a world of dreams, and chase dreams... and in the end, dreams undo us.''|Excerpt from ''Words to Would-be Apprentices''}}

==Contents==
Khelben Arunsun's book was a collection musings and lessoned aimed at his potential future pupils. The book was also a warning to  future wizards about the dangers of ''[[the Art]].''

==History==
By the late 14{{th}} century [[DR]], a copy of ''Words to Would-be Apprentices'' was collected in the annals of the citadel library of [[Candlekeep]] on the [[Sword Coast]].{{Cite web/The Candlekeep Collection}}
