Gobo was a village located in Miyama Province in Kozakura.
Gobo was a quaint and serene community of seventy-eight buildings, and was renowned for its picturesque and captivating landscape.
This charming setting made the village a favored destination for travelers, poets, artists, and nobility. To cater to these visitors, Gobo boasted a well-regarded inn and provided various services tailored to the needs of its distinguished guests. Besides its appeal to visitors, the village also sustained an active fishing industry and operated a mill.
As of , the villages jito was Fukada Shiro. He oversaw the shoen encompassing the village.
Fukada Shiro maintained a significant military presence of thirty-six samurai and sixty bushi.
