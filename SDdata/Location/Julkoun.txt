Julkoun was a hamlet on the Delimbiyr halfway between Daggerford and Secomber.
The pastoral hamlet was home to about forty households of stout farmers. The hamlet was filled with small gardens surrounded by low stone walls and hedgerows. The streets were grassy and often filled with grazing goats and sheep.
The hamlet was located along the banks of the Delimbiyr River, upstream from Laughing Hollow.
The village was originally the hamlet of Shining until Julkoun built a large stone mill and shrine to Chauntea in 1287 DR. 
The Jesters Pride an excellent inn. 
Julkouns Old Mill a large stone mill.  
Shining River Mill a clothyard.  
Yevershoulder a family of halflings.
