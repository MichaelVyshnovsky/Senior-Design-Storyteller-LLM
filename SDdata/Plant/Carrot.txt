'''Carrots''' were a type of hardy, root vegetable found in the Realms and often used in [[stew]]s and [[salad]]s.

==Description==
These vegetables grew to a much larger size on [[Toril]] compared to those on [[Earth]].{{Cite dragon/277/The New Adventures of Volo: Dragonwing Stew|94}}

==Availability==
Places in the Realms where carrots were known to be produced included:
* [[Cormyr]], particularly around the village of [[Eveningstar]]<ref>{{Cite dragon/157/Next Stop: Eveningstar|64}} and the town of [[Ghars]].<ref>
*The [[Oak-Father's Boon]] inn in [[Mosstone]] of [[Tethyr]] served a spicy carrot and [[ginger]] soup as one of its specialities.
* [[The North]], where carrots were cultivated in the walled abbey of [[Goldenfields]];
* [[North Faerûn]], where farmers in the [[Battledale]] and [[Mistledale]] regions cultivated carrots; In the [[Moonsea]] region, farmers in [[Elmwood]] cultivated carrots.{{Cite polyhedron/77/The Everwinking Eye: Moonsea Shores|7}}
* [[South Faerûn]], where they were cultivated in the [[Arnaden region]].
* The [[Western Heartlands]], such as in the [[gnome]] village of [[Anga Vled]].{{Cite dragon/269/The New Adventures of Volo: Hin Nobody Knows|86}}
* [[The Vast]], at the village and vale of [[Maskyr's Eye]]; carrots and other root vegetables grew well in the vale.{{Cite polyhedron/57/The Everwinking Eye: At Home in Maskyr's Eye|20}}

===Beyond Faerûn===
* Carrots were grown throughout much of [[Kara-Tur]].<ref>

==Usages==
[[File:Big stiff carrot.png|thumb]]
* A whole dried carrot was required to cast the ''[[detect lycanthrope]]'' [[spell]].{{Cite dragon/130/Arcane Lore: Magic from East to West|17}} Just a bit of a dried carrot acted as a [[material component]] for the spells [[darkvision (spell)|''darkvision'']], ''[[deeper darkvision]]'', and ''[[mass darkvision]]''.

==Notable Users==
* Carrots, grilled [[prawn]]s, [[bass]], and [[onion]]s on skewers were a commoners feast in Sembia and its city of [[Selgaunt]].
* [[Targos]]'s [[Trip and Shuffle]] tavern served a stew of meat, [[potato]]s and carrots in the late 15{{th}} century DR.
