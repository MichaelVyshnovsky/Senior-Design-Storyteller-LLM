'''Rhubarb''', or '''Redroot''',Page 88 of ''[[Ed Greenwood Presents Elminster's Forgotten Realms]]'' misidentifies this as being a fruit.

==Description==
These vegetables were short and leafy with bright red stalks. They were notable for being both sour and slightly sweet.<ref>

==Availability==
Places in the Realms where rhubarb was known to grow or be cultivated were:
* [[Northwest Faerûn]]: Wild rhubarb grew in the [[Delimbiyr Vale]]. And was cultivated in the gardens of [[Goldenfields]].
* [[Luiren]]: Rhubarb grew in the [[Long Forest]]. Along the southern rim of that forest it was cultivated and traded widely by the city of [[Fasruil]].
* The farmers of [[Gaj]] in the [[Vilhon Reach]] grew rhubarb making it the town's main dessert of choice.{{Cite web/theedverse/2022-12-15 - Gaj}}
* The [[Katakoro Plateau]].
* In [[Marsember/Xiousing|Xiousing]], a small [[Shou]] district in the city of [[Marsember]], rhubarb was grown by [[Shen's Herb Garden]].{{Cite dungeon/195/Backdrop: Xiousing|6}}
* Wild rhubarb was also known by some to grow in great numbers in areas of [[fey]] presence, serving as indication of a [[fey crossroad]] being near.<ref>{{Cite organized play/LFR/Lost Love|28}}

==Usages==
* The stalks of rhubarb were used in the filling of a Luiren pastry known as [[bouqthi]].
* Some used rhubarb in the making of [[incense]].
* Powdered rhubarb leaves were a [[material component]] for the [[spell]] ''[[Melf's acid arrow]]''.
* Some were known to cultivate rhubarb for medicinal purposes, such as an astringent or cathartic.<ref>
* Rhubarb could be used to make [[tea]].

==Notable Users==
===Businesses===
* [[Drover's Last Drink]], a tavern in [[Mosstone]] served a wide variety of handpies and bigger bowl-sized stuffed goodness. Rhubarb [[tart]]s and many-berry tarts were sold for one [[copper piece]] per serving of seven.

===Organizations===
* The [[Ironaxe Clan]] of [[dwarves]] were known to gather rhubarb, in addition to other wild foodstuffs, from the Delimbiyr Vale.

===Races & Sentient Creatures===
* Rhubarb was common in Elven cuisine.
