'''Anwat al-Makkar''' was a skilled [[sa'luk]] and sheikh of the [[House of Dhi'b]] tribe in the [[High Desert]] of [[west Zakhara]] around [[1367 DR]].{{AQ-time}}<ref name=LoF:AGtZ-p37">

==Personality==
Sly and cunning, Anwat was usually able to outmaneuver his enemies.<ref name=LoF:AGtZ-p37"/>

==Description==
Anwat was an old wizened man by 1367 DR. His gaunt frame earned him the nickname ''Tarkib al-Azam'' which translated from [[Midani language|Midani]] to mean "skeleton."<ref name=LoF:AGtZ-p37"/>

==Activities==
Anwat rode with [[Kori al-Zafiri]] to depose the sultan of [[Tajar]]. He organized several clans, numbering around 200 individuals each, to raid caravans passing through the south and south-central regions of the desert. Many of these caravans departed Tajar which was under the rule of Kori's son, [[Ali al-Hadd]].<ref name=LoF:AGtZ-p37"/>

He organized a series of fortresses scattered throughout the [[Range of the Marching Camels]]<ref group="note">The source does not specifically mention the Range of the Marching Camels, but it is assumed these are the mountains where the fortresses were located as there are no other mountain ranges found in the southern regions of the High Desert. to serve as treasure vaults. These fortresses were rumored to be hidden with magic.<ref name=LoF:AGtZ-p37"/>

==Relationships==
The sheikh allied himself with various clans over the years to suit his needs.<ref name=LoF:AGtZ-p37"/>
