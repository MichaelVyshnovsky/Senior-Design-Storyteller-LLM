Honeyed buns were a type of local bread dish from the city of Arrabar in the Vilhon Reach.
Honeyed buns were small-in-size handfoods covered in sweet honey, making them a cheap treat and popular morningfeast among the common folk of Arrabar.
Circa the , blind aging commoner Nimra Skolotti and her young daughter Mirolyn Skolotti liked to start their day with a single fresh honeyed bun from a local bakery in the Living District they loved in. A simple pleasure, the women split a bun as they took care of daily chores.
