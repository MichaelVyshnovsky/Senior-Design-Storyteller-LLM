'''Billhooks''' were a type of agricultural tool in the Realms that could double as an improvised weapon.

==Description==
Billhooks had wooden shafts and [[iron]] heads that consisted of a hook and a sharp, barbed tip.

==Availability==
* [[Aurora's Whole Realms Catalogue (in-universe)|''Aurora's Whole Realms Catalogue'']] sold billhooks, as well as molds for smiths to make billhook hooks for 7 [[silver piece]]s a piece.<ref> The catalogue advertised the tool as being useful for removing the loose thatch off of a roof in the case of a fire and for attacking [[vermin]] that nested in thatched roofs.
* The [[Iron Bull Smithy]] in [[Ravens Bluff]] sold billhooks for 5 [[gold piece]]s.<ref>{{Cite polyhedron/103/The Living City: The Horse Businesses|16}}
* The [[Moonlight Pawnshop]], a pawnbrokerage in Ravens Bluff, sold billhooks alongside other common farming tools.<ref>{{Cite polyhedron/105/The Living City: Misti's Moonlight Pawnshop|13}}

[[File:Desert of Desolation - Farmer.jpg|thumb|273px|''A farmer holding a billhook, from the D&D Miniature's set "Desert of Desolation"'']]
==History==
The combination polearm known as the [[bill-guisarme]] was developed from this tool. 

==Notable Users==
* [[Harginn]] were often known to fight with long billhooks.<ref>
* Some in the [[Blade Kingdoms]] were known to use their billhooks as weapons.

A billhook featured prominently in the story, [[The Adventures of Camber Fosrick|''"The Adventure of the Battledale Billhook"'']].
