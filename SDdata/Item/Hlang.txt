A hlang was a Chultan oval-shaped, hide shield, most common in the cities of Mezro
An oval hlang was  wide and between  tall. It consisted of animal hide&mdashoften dinosaur hide&mdashfitted over a wooden frame. The shield was designed to be collapsible the hide could easily be removed, rolled up, and used as a chair or pillow, while the frame could be folded up for easy storage.
A hlang was approximately equivalent to a typical large wooden shield in terms of weight and defensive capabilities.
* Priests in the Church of Eshowdow.
* High-ranking priests in the Church of Ubtao.
* Members of the Tabaxi tribe were particularly known to be proficient in the use of hlangs.
