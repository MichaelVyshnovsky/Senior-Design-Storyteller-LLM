Eccles cakes were sweet and fruity handfoods from the hamlet of Liams Hold.
Eccles cakes were a type of small delectable pastry stuffed with mincemeats and dried fruits. They traditionally were eaten during the cold months of the year accompanied by a glass of hot mulled wine.
These small cakes were a staple of Nells Pie Shop of Liams Hold circa 1372 DR.
