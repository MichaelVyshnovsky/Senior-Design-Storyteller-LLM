The '''symbol of Chelsinara''' was a symbol worn by some followers of [[Oghma]], the Lord of Knowledge. The symbol was named for [[Chelsinara]], an important early priestess of Oghma.<ref name=FaA-p133">

==Description==
The symbol depicted two cupped hands with thumbs touching and fingers pointing upward.<ref name=FaA-p133"/>

==Usage==
The symbol meant "I learn" and was used as a badge by followers of Oghma, priests and lay persons alike, to indicate their membership of the faith. It was also sewn in braid on each breast of the crimson or purple [[kantlara]], vests worn by the clergy.<ref name=FaA-p133"/>
