Bee was a personal cantrip that wizards-in-training could learn. It summoned an angry honey bee to a spot chosen by the caster.
This cantrip conjured a single honey bee that appeared at a spot within The original description of this spell in Dragon #60 used the 1-edition nomenclature 1", meaning  indoors and  outdoors. This was simplified to the shorter range in Wizards Spell Compendium Volume Four. and visible to the caster (that is, it could not be inside a pouch or under clothing, for example). The insect was irritated by the summoning and was highly likely to sting if it appeared on or very close to a living creature.
This cantrip required verbal and somatic components to cast. The caster focused their vision on the desired target spot, made a soft buzzing sound, and traced a wandering path in the air similar to the flight of a bee.
