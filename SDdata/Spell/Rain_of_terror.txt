Rain of terror was a wizard spell from the school of evocation unique to the Domains of Dread.Wizards Spell Compendium Volume Three marks Rain of terror as a Zakharan spell from the Al-Qadim campaign setting. Wizards Spell Compendium Volume Four corrects this to a Ravenloft spell in its errata p. 1152.
This spell created a cursed rain storm that took effect over the course of half an hour. The spell would begin immediately if storm clouds were present at the time of casting. The storm lasted for at least one hour, and longer depending on the skill of the caster, covering an area of  in a circle centered on the caster.
The caster was able to pick the nature of the cursed rainfall based on their skill. Potential choices included black putrid water, ash, steaming blood, toads, dead bats, snakes, and spiders. Half of the living creatures that rained down survived the impact with the ground. Though they were harmless, the living creatures added an element of terror to the storm.
This spell only required verbal and somatic components to cast.
Rain of terror was among the spells collected by the infamous Strahd von Zarovich.
